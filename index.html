Projects

ExpresultHub

When conducting reproducibility research, it is often necessary to run the same program multiple times and record various server metrics, such as CPU utilization, memory usage, and other relevant data. ExpresultHub is designed to assist researchers in automating the recording of these inputs. Researchers simply need to specify the program commands, the metrics to monitor, and the number of iterations in our script. Our system will automatically log this data in the background and store it in JSON files. Additionally, we provide a GUI with charts to help users visualize the recorded data effectively.

  • React
  • Node.js

WellnessWave

WellnessWave is designed to assist individuals who want to maintain their health by tracking their daily diet, exercise, and changes in weight and measurements. About Me

My passion for Computer Science began during my undergraduate studies in Data Science at the University of Liverpool. There, I delved deep into data processing, data mining, and data analysis, sparking a desire to apply my knowledge to real-world challenges.

Driven by this ambition, I joined the University of Hong Kong as a research assistant from November 2021 to July 2022, under the supervision of Dr. Lequan Yu and Dr. Shujun Wang. My research focused on multi-task self-supervised learning in 3D medical image analysis, leading to a publication as the first author in CMMCA 2022 in conjunction with MICCAI 2022, where it was awarded Best Paper.

In addition to research, I sought to experience the industry side of data work, leading me to a data engineer intern role at GoLaxy in the summer of 2022. There, I worked on news data mining and sentiment analysis, further broadening my understanding of data's impact.

However, through these experiences, I realized that while many technologies are well-developed in the academic world, their real-world application often falls short. This insight led me to ask myself, How can technology not just solve problems in research, but enhance human experiences? This question fueled my transition into software development.

To gain practical experience, I participated in the Summer of Reproducibility, an open-source initiative, where I was mentored by Dr. Anjo Vahldiek-Oberwagner from Intel Labs. Together, we developed software to monitor multi-experiments and multi-iterations in machine performance, aiding researchers in improving reproducibility.

With a passion for bridging the gap between research and application, I pursued a master degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Waterloo. Throughout this journey, I specialized in software development, honing my skills in creating robust and scalable solutions that bring cutting-edge research to life. Now, I am seeking opportunities as a Software Engineer, where I can leverage my technical expertise and innovative mindset to develop impactful software solutions that address real-world challenges.

I am particularly interested in roles that allow me to work on diverse and challenging projects, whether it be in web development, AI integration, or system architecture. I place a high value on the long-term prospects of my career and am deeply committed to roles that offer not only immediate engagement but also significant potential for personal growth and development. I am focused on aligning myself with a company that is forward-thinking, with a strong vision for the future, and that offers opportunities for continuous learning and professional advancement.
